# Labeling Sessions and Labeling Schemas

**Labeling Sessions** and **Labeling Schemas** are components of the [MLflow GenAI Data Model](/concepts/mlflow-genai-data-model.md) that support the collection of human feedback and ground-truth annotations on [Traces](/concepts/traces.md) generated by a generative AI application. Together, they provide a structured workflow for domain experts to review application outputs and record quality assessments.

## Overview

In the MLflow experiment data model, human labeling data is organized under each experiment. Labeling sessions are queues of traces that need expert review, and labeling schemas define the precise questions and answer formats that reviewers use when providing assessments. This separation of concerns allows teams to collect consistent, high-quality feedback on complex or ambiguous cases and to build ground-truth datasets for systematic evaluation. ^[concepts-data-model-databricks-on-aws.md]

## Labeling Sessions

A **labeling session** is a container that groups selected traces for human review. Each session:

- Queues the traces that require expert attention.
- Captures the assessments that reviewers produce.
- Associates the assessments with the corresponding traces in the experiment.

Labeling sessions serve two primary purposes:

1. **Collect expert feedback** on outputs that automated [[scorers]] cannot reliably judge – for example, nuanced reasoning, domain-specific correctness, or edge cases.
2. **Create ground-truth data** that can be used to build or extend [Evaluation Datasets](/concepts/evaluation-datasets.md), enabling more accurate offline quality evaluation.

The resulting assessments (both feedback and expectations) are attached to the traces and become available for analysis, model tuning, and quality monitoring. ^[concepts-data-model-databricks-on-aws.md]

## Labeling Schemas

A **labeling schema** defines the structure of the assessments collected during a labeling session. It ensures that every domain expert applies the same criteria when reviewing traces, which is essential for consistency across reviewers and over time.

A schema specifies:

- **Questions** that reviewers must answer about a trace (e.g., "Is this response accurate?").
- **Valid response types** for each question, such as:
  - Thumbs-up / thumbs-down (binary).
  - Numeric scales (e.g., 1–5).
  - Free-text comments.

By enforcing a uniform assessment structure, labeling schemas make it possible to aggregate and compare human ratings across traces and sessions. They also allow the MLflow system to store the results as structured [Assessments](/concepts/assessments.md) that can be queried and analyzed programmatically. ^[concepts-data-model-databricks-on-aws.md]

## How They Work Together

A typical workflow proceeds as follows:

1. **Traces** are collected from development runs or production monitoring.
2. A data scientist or engineer creates a **labeling session**, selecting a subset of traces that need human review.
3. A **labeling schema** is attached to the session, defining the questions and response types the reviewers will use.
4. Domain experts access the traces through the [Review App](/concepts/mlflow-review-app.md) (a web UI) and provide assessments according to the schema.
5. The completed assessments are stored as feedback or expectations on the original traces.

The labeled traces then feed back into the evaluation lifecycle – for example, by becoming part of an evaluation dataset used in `mlflow.genai.evaluate()`. ^[concepts-data-model-databricks-on-aws.md]

## Related Concepts

- [Traces](/concepts/traces.md) – The application execution records that are queued for review.
- [Assessments](/concepts/assessments.md) – The quality measurements (feedback and expectations) that result from labeling.
- [Evaluation Datasets](/concepts/evaluation-datasets.md) – Curated test cases; ground truth from labeling can be added as expectations.
- [Review App](/concepts/mlflow-review-app.md) – The web interface domain experts use to label traces.
- Domain Experts – The human labelers who provide assessments.
- [[Scorers]] – Automated evaluators; labeling complements them by handling cases that require human judgment.
